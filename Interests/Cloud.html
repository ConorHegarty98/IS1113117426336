What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing is an information technology (IT) paradigm that enables ubiquitous access to shared pools of configurable system resources and higher-level services that can be rapidly provisioned with minimal management effort, often over the Internet. Cloud computing relies on sharing of resources to achieve coherence and economies of scale, similar to a public utility.

Benefits of Cloud Computing

  • Control Costs-The cloud enables you to leverage the power and performance of enterprise-grade IT infrastructure without the capital expenditure of managing and maintaining your own hardware. Its pay-as-you-go model can also help you control costs.
  • Improve Security-In a cloud environment, security is a shared responsibility. Your cloud hosting provider builds in resiliency and protection at the infrastructure level to minimize the risk of a breach, and can help you address compliance requirements.
  • Pay per use: Compute resources are measured at a granular level, enabling users to pay only for the resources and workloads they use.
  • Prepare for Growth-The cloud offers a flexible way for businesses to accommodate both unpredictable demand and planned growth more cost effectively. It can also be used as a platform for delivering new services and accelerating your time-to-market.
  • Focus on Innovation-The cloud enables you to focus on running your business, instead of your IT. Use your resources to design and deploy innovative applications and services that will drive business results and differentiate you from the competition.
